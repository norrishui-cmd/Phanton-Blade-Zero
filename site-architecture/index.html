What a Phantom Blade Zero Guide Site Should Include

A high-quality guide site for this game type needs more than news posts. It needs combat learning paths, boss strategies, weapon and Phantom Edge testing, spoiler-safe story coverage, route maps, build logic, and transparent update rules.

Core Content Elements

Phantom Blade Zero sits at the intersection of fast action RPG combat, wuxia/kungfupunk presentation, boss-driven mastery, and story-route curiosity. The site should therefore cover twelve content pillars: release and platforms, beginner onboarding, combat mechanics, weapons and Phantom Edges, bosses and elites, maps and regions, walkthrough and side quests, endings, story and lore, builds, items and upgrades, and technical settings.

Pre-Launch vs Launch Content

Before launch, pages should focus on confirmed public material, official terminology, and useful templates. After launch, the same pages should be upgraded with tested data: exact item locations, boss move names, route screenshots, quest outcomes, and patch-specific notes.

Quality Rules for New URLs

  • Each URL must answer a distinct search intent.
  • Names of bosses, weapons, quests, regions, endings, and rewards must come from official material or direct gameplay verification.
  • Pages with only a placeholder should stay out of the sitemap or carry a noindex rule until expanded.
  • Every child page should link to its parent hub and at least two related guides.

Missing Boards Added

This update adds hub pages for maps, items, achievements, settings, PC performance, controller setup, accessibility, glossary, updates, and content architecture so the site has the right skeleton before detailed launch data arrives.
