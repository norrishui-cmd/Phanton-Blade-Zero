Why This Wiki Uses an Official-Source-First Editorial Policy

Pre-release games attract placeholders, rumor, mistranslation, and trailer speculation. This wiki uses a source hierarchy to keep those signals from becoming permanent facts.

What Is Confirmed

S-GAME and platform pages establish release, platforms, story framing, languages, and advertised systems. Official videos can confirm what is shown, but not hidden inputs or final names. After launch, menus, tutorials, item text, reward screens, and patch notes become the primary mechanical evidence. Community reports suggest tests rather than replacing them.

What It Means for Players

Every time-sensitive answer shows a checked date. Conflicting official listings are described instead of silently resolved. Unnamed bosses, weapons, quests, and endings remain outside the sitemap until a page can provide a concrete, verifiable answer.

Source and Verification

This update is based on Primary-source baseline. It separates confirmed statements from inferences and records the publication date so later announcements or retail testing can replace outdated information.

Next Update

The related about hub will be revised when S-GAME, PlayStation, Steam, the dedicated State of Play, or the release build supplies a concrete change. Rumors, retailer placeholders, community tags, and edited footage are not treated as final mechanical proof.
