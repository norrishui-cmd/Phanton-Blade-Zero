Phantom Blade Zero Uses a Semi-Open World Structure

S-GAME has described Phantom Blade Zero as a semi-open world made from multiple maps of reasonable size, handcrafted and populated with different activities.

What Is Confirmed

That wording suggests more exploration freedom than a strictly linear corridor, but it does not prove one seamless world. Fast travel, loading transitions, backtracking rules, day-night systems, map completion, and the size of individual regions remain incomplete before release.

What It Means for Players

Beginner guides will avoid promising unrestricted travel. Region pages will be activated only after official or in-game names are verified, then connect routes, bosses, quests, items, and collectibles. This prevents trailer locations from becoming inaccurate permanent labels.

This update is based on S-GAME PlayStation Blog introduction. It separates confirmed statements from inferences and records the publication date so later announcements or retail testing can replace outdated information.
