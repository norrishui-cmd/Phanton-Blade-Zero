Trust

Sources and Verification Policy

The wiki separates confirmed public information, tracked pre-launch questions, and launch-only details that require direct verification.

Preferred Sources

  • Official S-GAME and Phantom Blade Zero pages.
  • Platform store pages for PC and PlayStation details.
  • Official trailers, gameplay demos, and publisher posts.
  • In-game menus, tutorials, item descriptions, and patch notes after release.

What Not to Publish as Fact

Do not invent boss names, weapons, Phantom Edges, quest outcomes, endings, map region names, item names, achievements, system requirements, or performance settings. Those pages should remain hubs until official or tested data exists.

Update Workflow

When a source changes, update the affected hub, add a note to updates, refresh the sitemap lastmod date, and keep old assumptions out of the copy.

Current Primary References

The editorial baseline uses the official PlayStation store for date and PS5 features, the official Steam page for PC availability, weapon counts, Phantom Edges, story, and technology, and S-GAME's PlayStation Blog introduction for The Order, the semi-open world, Kungfupunk, and combat direction. A 2024 PlayStation hands-on report supports the public block, parry, and dodge descriptions.

Freshness and Corrections

Time-sensitive pages show a checked date. If two official listings disagree, the page describes the difference instead of silently choosing one. Retail testing must record platform and patch; community reports can suggest a test but do not replace reproducible evidence. Corrections should update affected internal links and sitemap dates in the same deployment.
